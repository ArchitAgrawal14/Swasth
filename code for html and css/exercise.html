Yoga Poses

Downward Dog

How to do: Start on your hands and knees, lift your hips towards the ceiling, forming an inverted V-shape.

When to do: Morning or evening.

Benefits: Stretches and strengthens the entire body, improves digestion.

Precautions: Avoid if you have wrist or shoulder injuries.

Tree Pose

How to do: Stand on one leg, place the sole of the other foot on the inner thigh or calf, bring hands to prayer position.

When to do: Anytime for balance and focus.

Benefits: Improves balance, strengthens thighs, and calms the mind.

Precautions: Avoid if you have knee or ankle issues.

Warrior Pose

How to do: Step one foot forward, bend the knee, extend arms overhead.

When to do: During a yoga practice for strength and endurance.

Benefits: Strengthens legs, opens hips, and improves balance.

Precautions: Be cautious with knee alignment; avoid overextending.

Child's Pose

How to do: Kneel on the mat, sit back on heels, stretch arms forward, lower the torso.

When to do: As a resting pose during a yoga session.

Benefits: Relieves stress, stretches the back, and promotes relaxation.

Precautions: Avoid if you have knee or ankle issues.

Cobra Pose

How to do: Lie on the stomach, place hands under shoulders, lift the chest while keeping the pelvis on the floor.

When to do: In the morning to awaken the spine.

Benefits: Strengthens the back, opens the chest, and improves posture.

Precautions: Avoid if you have back or neck injuries.

Fitness Poses

Push-ups

How to do: Start in a plank position, lower your body towards the ground by bending your elbows, then push back up.

When to do: Anytime during your workout routine.

Benefits: Strengthens chest, shoulders, and triceps.

Precautions: Keep your body in a straight line, avoid arching your back.

Squats

How to do: Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, bend your knees, and lower your hips as if you are sitting back into a chair.

When to do: During leg workouts for strength and toning.

Benefits: Strengthens quads, hamstrings, and glutes.

Precautions: Keep knees aligned with toes, don't let them extend beyond toes.

Lunges

How to do: Step one foot forward, bend both knees, lower the body, and then push back up.

When to do: As part of a lower body workout routine.

Benefits: Works the muscles in the legs and buttocks.

Precautions: Ensure proper knee alignment, avoid overextending.

Plank

How to do: Hold a push-up position with the body in a straight line from head to heels.

When to do: Core strengthening during a full-body workout.

Benefits: Strengthens core muscles, improves posture.

Precautions: Keep the body straight, avoid sagging or lifting hips.

Jumping Jacks

How to do: Start with feet together, jump and spread arms and legs, then return to the starting position.

When to do: As a cardio exercise during warm-up or a full-body workout.

Benefits: Increases heart rate, improves cardiovascular fitness.

Precautions: Land softly to protect joints, especially the knees.
